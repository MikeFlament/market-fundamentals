E-MINI NQ · CME · 2026
4H · HTF STRUCTURE
Key Concepts
01 · BULLISH FVG
4-Hour Fair Value Gap
A bullish imbalance on the 4hr chart defines our primary area of interest. Price is expected to return to this inefficiency before continuing higher.
02 · LIQUIDITY
Sweep of Key Low
A liquidity sweep occurs below a key low resting inside our 4hr FVG level — confirming institutional interest and providing the fuel for a reversal.
Process
01 Identify the 4hr Bullish Fair Value Gap — this is your anchor level for the trade. Mark the high and low of the FVG range clearly on your chart.
02 Confirm a liquidity sweep of a key low sitting inside or near the FVG. The sweep validates that smart money is accumulating at this level.
03 Once both conditions are met, drop to the 15m chart to map targets and risk levels. Never enter on the HTF alone — wait for lower timeframe confirmation.
15M · DRAW ON LIQUIDITY
Key Concepts
01 · FIRST TARGET
Unfilled 15m FVG
The nearest draw on liquidity is the unfilled 15-minute Fair Value Gap above entry. This is your first profit-taking or breakeven level.
02 · RISK MANAGEMENT
Remove Risk Early
The 15m FVG acts as a signal to reduce position size or move stop to breakeven — locking in gains while letting the remainder run.
Target Identification
01 Identify the first obvious draw on liquidity above the 4hr FVG entry zone. In this example, an unfilled 15m FVG sits just above the entry area.
02 Use the 15m FVG as a breakeven trigger or partial profit point. When price reaches this level, consider closing 50% of the position.
03 Mark higher draws on liquidity for remaining position targets. Look for equal highs, previous swing highs, or higher-timeframe FVGs above.
3M · ENTRY MODEL
Key Concepts
01 · TIMEFRAME DRILL
Lower Timeframe Entry
Once price hits the 4hr FVG, drop to the 5m, 3m, 2m, or 1m charts. Look for the highest timeframe inversion that has formed — do not skip timeframes.
02 · INVERSION FVG
3-Minute IFVG
A bearish FVG that gets violated to the upside becomes an Inversion FVG (IFVG). Price returning to this zone is your entry signal for the long.
Entry Execution
01 Confirm price has reached the 4hr HTF key level (the FVG). This is non-negotiable — do not look for entries before this is satisfied.
02 Drop to lower timeframes (5m → 3m → 2m → 1m) and find the highest TF inversion present. In this example, the 3-Minute IFVG is the qualifying entry signal.
03 Wait for the candle to close into the IFVG zone, then place the long order. A closed candle — not a wick — is required to confirm the signal is valid.
04 Stop loss sits below the 4hr FVG low. First target is the 15m FVG identified in Step 2. Risk-to-reward should be at minimum 1:2 before taking the trade.
